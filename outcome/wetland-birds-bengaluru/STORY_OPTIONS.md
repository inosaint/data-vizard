# Story Direction Options

## Posture

`Data-art-led explorer`

## Rejected Set

The user rejected the first option set: Waterline Mosaic, Fifty Portraits Five Guilds, and Commons Aviary.

The user then requested inspiration from public visualizations. See `INSPIRATION.md` for the research scan.

## Candidate A: Generous Wetland Archive

Inspired by Macaulay Library and generous collection interfaces. All 50 species appear immediately as an uneven image field, grouped by checklist category with very light labels.

Strength: Browseability is built into the composition, not delegated to search.

Risk: It must avoid becoming a conventional gallery grid.

Caveat: Category density reflects checklist membership, not abundance.

## Candidate B: Hidden Plate Reveal

Inspired by Science Museum Group's Never Been Seen. The first view is a quiet plate of softened or masked image apertures; focus reveals one species sharply with name, category, Wikipedia link, and Commons attribution.

Strength: Discovery becomes the core interaction.

Risk: Too much concealment could frustrate users who want to scan the whole collection.

Caveat: The reveal treatment is an interface device, not a claim about rarity or hiddenness.

## Candidate C: Checklist Canopy

Inspired by OneZoom, but not presented as taxonomy. The five checklist categories become branching paths or ribs; each species is a leaf-like image node.

Strength: Strong spatial identity and an intuitive way to move through groups.

Risk: Branching can accidentally imply phylogenetic or family hierarchy if labels are not careful.

Caveat: Branches encode checklist categories only.

## Candidate D: Public Field Book

Inspired by Biodiversity Heritage Library. The piece feels like a contemporary natural-history spread: image fragments, source links, tiny license marks, and category marginalia.

Strength: Best match for public-domain/archive energy and restrained text.

Risk: Could become too static if interaction is underdesigned.

Caveat: Historical field-book styling is an aesthetic direction, not a claim about source age.

## Candidate E: Radar Without Routes

Selected by the user and revised after research. Inspired by BirdCast's luminous nocturnal visual language, but adapted carefully to a Bengaluru map. Selected migrant, winter-visitor, and seasonal wetland species become month-by-month observation pulses over Bengaluru lake and habitat localities.

Strength: Atmospheric and kinetic while remaining source-linked.

Risk: Needs very clear restraint so viewers do not read it as radar, routes, abundance, or real-time migration traffic.

Caveat: Motion encodes public seasonal sighting records, not flight paths, live radar, or true abundance.
