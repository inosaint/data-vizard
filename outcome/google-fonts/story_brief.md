# Story Brief: The Catalog Is Global, But Still Latin-First

## Selected Direction

- Analyst direction: `The Catalog Is Global, But Still Latin-First`
- Posture: `Data-art-led`
- Language load: `minimal copy`

## Narrative Frame

Visual-led comparison. The piece should read as a field of script coverage rather than a written argument. The visual should first suggest breadth, then expose how sharply the catalog concentrates around Latin support and a thinner multilingual long tail.

## Main Question

What does the Google Fonts catalog look like when each script subset is treated as part of a visible coverage landscape rather than a dropdown list?

## Main Observation

The catalog appears globally expansive, but the coverage structure is highly concentrated: `latin` and `latin-ext` dominate, a few additional subsets carry real depth, and most scripts sit in a much thinner tail.

## Supporting Evidence

1. `latin` appears in 1,923 of 1,938 families.
2. `latin-ext` appears in 1,508 families.
3. The next subset, `vietnamese`, falls to 511 families.
4. Only 15 families in this snapshot omit `latin`.
5. A few workhorse families stretch across many subsets, while 327 families support only one subset.

## Optional Text

- A tiny note calling out the drop from `latin-ext` to `vietnamese`
- A compact disclosure entry point for method and source notes

## Annotation Priorities

- Make the first few coverage tiers obvious
- Preserve the visual impression of the long tail
- Let exact values appear on focus instead of labeling everything upfront
- Keep caveats near the disclosure surface, not as a large always-open panel

## Designer Handoff

Treat this as a typographic landscape or archive wall, not as a control-heavy explorer. The first screen should be mostly visual. If interaction is used, it should reveal structure, not just add filters because interfaces usually have filters.

Spatial archetype: `ledger strip` meeting `cabinet wall`. Avoid `hero + card stack`, right-rail detail panels, and repeated tile-box grammar in the tail.
