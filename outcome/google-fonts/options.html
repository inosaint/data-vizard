Google Fonts coverage can read as a field, a poster, or a specimen wall.

All three directions keep the story data-art-led and copy-light. The difference is where the meaning lives: in a spatial coverage field, in ranked typographic contrast, or in a quieter archival matrix.

Option A

Orbit Field

Studio gloss + diagrammatic atlas. A radial field where subset families cluster by coverage depth, making the dominance of Latin feel immediate before the long tail resolves.

  • Focal point: the steep drop from the central coverage tiers to the outer ring.
  • Rendering: D3/SVG or positioned HTML marks.
  • Risk avoided: does not read like a spreadsheet ranking.
Option B

Signal Drop Poster

Minimal brutalist + editorial poster. A sparse ranked composition that turns the subset drop-off into one dramatic typographic chart.

  • Focal point: the visual break between `latin-ext` and `vietnamese`.
  • Rendering: HTML/CSS/SVG.
  • Risk avoided: does not bury the main comparison inside atmospheric clustering.

Coverage Cabinet
