# Product Context: Google Fonts Coverage Field

## Audience

This artifact is primarily for local workflow testing inside the Data Vizard plugin, but it should still read like a public-facing exploratory piece for people interested in typography, language coverage, and catalog structure.

## Purpose

Show the shape of Google Fonts coverage without pretending the dataset measures popularity or quality. The piece should make the catalog feel broad at first glance, then reveal how quickly support concentrates around Latin and a smaller set of multilingual workhorse fonts.

## Personality

Calm, deliberate, and visually led. More gallery wall than dashboard. More typographic specimen field than product browser.

## Design Principles

- Let the visual field carry the first impression.
- Keep copy minimal and precise.
- Make the coverage drop-off legible without turning the page into a ranking table.
- Preserve a sense of catalog scale and long-tail rarity.
- Avoid generic app chrome, KPI strips, and heavy filter rails.

## Anti-References

- Do not make this look like a font picker.
- Do not make this look like a SaaS dashboard.
- Do not imply usage share, market share, or linguistic importance from support counts alone.

## Accessibility Goals

- Strong contrast in all primary states.
- Keyboard-reachable focus targets for any interactive marks.
- A reduced-motion path for any reveal or clustering transitions.
- Tooltips and detail text that expose exact counts without requiring color alone.
