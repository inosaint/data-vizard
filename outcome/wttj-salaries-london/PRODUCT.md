# WTTJ Salaries London

## Audience

General audience exploring how salary progression differs across tech and business roles in London.

## Purpose

Build an explanatory scrollytelling piece from Welcome to the Jungle's role-level salary trajectory data.

## Personality

Analytical, clear, and evidence-led rather than hype-driven.

## Narrative Posture

Explanatory

## Intent Brief

Use the salary trajectory dataset to explain how career pay progression differs by role, with emphasis on where early salaries, mid-career acceleration, and senior ceilings diverge.

## Accessibility Goals

Readable charts, clear comparative framing, and restrained copy that explains the main takeaway without forcing dense annotation.

## Design Principles

- Let the salary curves and comparisons do most of the explanatory work.
- Highlight role differences with honest comparisons inside a clearly legible retro race metaphor.
- Keep visible language focused on the claim each scroll step needs.
- Treat hover states as required for all data marks that invite inspection.
- Keep visible source attribution linked to the public Welcome to the Jungle salaries page.
- Preserve pixel-faithful broadcast styling only where it does not compromise readability.
- Prefer a scoreboard-heavy broadcast frame over a full illustrated stadium.
- Let runner sprites carry the race metaphor, with role names separated from the lanes for legibility.
- Use the HUD as a live ranking board that updates with the active race step.
- Keep all selected role names permanently visible down the left side of the race field.

## Anti-References

- Generic dashboard shells
- HR blog infographic styling
- Overwritten explainer pages with long intro copy

## Default Approach To Avoid

Hero plus card-stack scrollytelling with shallow takeaways and generic salary-ranking charts.

## Selected Moodboard

NES literal race broadcast: side-on stadium scene, pixel-lane geometry, chunky scoreboard HUD, and compressed retro color blocks inspired by the user's Olympic Gold references.
