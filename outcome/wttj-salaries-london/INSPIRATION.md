## Additional research

- `Track & Field` was originally released by Konami in `1983` as an arcade game, then adapted into Famicom/NES variants later.
- `Olympic Gold` was released in `1992` for Sega platforms, including Genesis, Master System, and Game Gear, not for the NES.
- The `Track & Field` gameplay format is especially relevant because contemporary descriptions emphasize a horizontal side-scrolling race view, a large scoreboard, and a packed audience in the background.
- The `Olympic Gold` lineage contributes the later broadcast-style scoreboard framing, official-Olympics presentation language, and flatter 8-bit/16-bit lane scene visible in the user screenshots.

## Transferable patterns to borrow

- Side-on race broadcast instead of top-down or abstract-track metaphor
- Sticky stadium scene that stays pinned while scroll updates the race state
- Chunky scoreboard HUD carrying stage, timing, and source information
- Deliberate pixel compression and lane-striping rather than polished editorial gradients
- Runners separating across a flat horizontal field so breakaway is legible at a glance
- A high, compressed HUD band above the race field rather than floating labels scattered around the scene
- Crowd and infield as shallow pixel bands, not detailed illustration, so the race lanes remain the focal point

## What not to copy literally

- Faux timing claims that imply real race speed rather than salary progression
- Decorative HUD clutter that competes with role names, values, or hover states
- Game scoring language that suggests winning by performance instead of pay trajectory
- National flags or Olympic branding that imply official affiliation

## Design implications

- The selected visual family is now `NES literal`, not warm editorial athletics
- The sticky device should feel like a pinned retro race broadcast
- Salary meaning should be carried by horizontal progress, lane labels, hover values, and stage framing
- Any retro surface treatment must still preserve source linkage and inspectable values
- The screen should likely resolve into a stacked broadcast frame: HUD band at top, shallow crowd/infield bands, then the race lanes
- The artifact should borrow `Track & Field` composition logic more than literal `Olympic Gold` branding
