# Product Context: World Cup Finishing Efficiency Field

## Audience

This artifact is primarily for local workflow testing inside the Data Vizard plugin, but it should still read like a public-facing piece for people who enjoy tournament football, stats culture, and visual sports storytelling.

## Purpose

Show that shot volume alone did not define finishing success in this group-stage-heavy snapshot. The piece should make scoring efficiency feel uneven, vivid, and slightly unstable across teams rather than presenting a flat ranking table.

## Personality

Charged, graphic, and visually led. More tournament poster than dashboard. More performance field than standings page.

## Narrative Posture

- `Data-art-led`

## Design Principles

- Let the visual field carry the first reading.
- Keep visible copy minimal and sharp.
- Make high-efficiency and low-efficiency teams legible without turning the page into a stats dashboard.
- Preserve volatility: this is a short sample, not a final truth about team quality.
- Avoid sports-app chrome, standings tables, filter rails, and detached detail panels.

## Anti-References

- Do not make this look like a betting site.
- Do not make this look like a live match dashboard.
- Do not make this look like a FIFA stats table with styling on top.

## Accessibility Goals

- Strong contrast in major visual states.
- Keyboard-reachable targets for any interactive marks.
- A reduced-motion path for all transitions.
- Exact values available through hover, focus, or nearby reveal without relying on color alone.

## Intent Brief

- who this piece is for:
  - viewers who like football and can read simple comparative stats but do not want a spreadsheet-like interface
- what they should understand:
  - some teams converted modest shot totals into excellent scoring returns, while others wasted huge attacking volume
- how it should feel:
  - taut, competitive, and visually decisive
- focal point:
  - the gap between efficient finishers near the top and wasteful volume teams near the bottom
- default approach to avoid:
  - sports dashboard, standings table, or generic card-grid team explorer
- aesthetic families to explore first:
  - `Constructivist signal`
  - `Editorial poster`
  - `Minimal brutalist`
- color attitude:
  - favor hot contrast with one cold counter-accent; avoid polite blue-neutral sports UI
- first screen attitude:
  - `studio`
