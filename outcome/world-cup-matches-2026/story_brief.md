# Story Brief: Efficient Attacks

## Selected Direction

- Analyst direction: `The Most Efficient Attacks Were Not Always The Biggest Favorites`
- Posture: `Data-art-led`
- Language load: `minimal copy`

## Narrative Frame

Visual-led performance field. The piece should feel like a tournament print surface where teams separate by how brutally or wastefully they turned shots into goals.

## Main Question

Which teams made the most of their chances in this short World Cup snapshot?

## Main Observation

The strongest finishers were not defined by raw shot volume alone. A few teams turned compact attacking output into goals at a much sharper rate, while others piled up attempts with very little payoff.

## Supporting Evidence

1. Netherlands scored `10` goals from `40` shots, a `0.25` goals-per-shot rate.
2. Japan scored `7` from `29`, `0.241` goals per shot.
3. Argentina scored `8` from `34`, `0.235` goals per shot.
4. Türkiye took `71` shots, the highest total among teams with at least three matches, but scored only `3`, a `0.042` goals-per-shot rate.
5. Panama scored `0` goals from `32` shots.

## Caveat

This is a short sample built mostly from group-stage matches. It shows performance in this snapshot, not a stable measure of overall team quality.

## Designer Handoff

Treat this as a competitive performance field, not a sports dashboard. The first screen should be mostly marks, ranks, and team identity. Avoid standings-table grammar, stat-card stacks, control rails, and remote selected-state panels.

Spatial archetype: `poster field` or `signal board`. Avoid `hero + component stack`, standings-table UI, and detached detail surfaces.
