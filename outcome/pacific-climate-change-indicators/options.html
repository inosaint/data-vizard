Pacific Tourism, Seen Beside Rainfall

Three possible visual directions for comparing tourist arrivals with precipitation anomalies across Pacific countries and territories.

A. Split Timeline

Aligned country timelines

Each selected country gets the same time axis: arrivals on top, rainfall anomaly below. This is the clearest form for reading comparison year by year.

Arrivals Wetter Drier
  • Strongest for precise country-by-country comparison.
  • Works well with a country selector and small multiples.
  • Risk: less dramatic as a first impression.
B. Matrix Explorer

Country rows with compact signals

A dense comparison board shows each country as a row: arrival trend, rainfall strip, and coverage indicator. Readers can scan patterns quickly.

Trend Wet Dry
  • Strongest for scanning all countries at once.
  • Good fit for a dashboard-style artifact.
  • Risk: needs careful labeling to avoid feeling cramped.
C. Focus Pair

Pick two places to compare

The page centers on a two-country comparison. It gives more room to annotations, tooltips, and the caveat that rainfall is context.

Arrivals Rain context Coverage
  • Strongest for close reading and annotation.
  • Good if readers should compare a chosen pair deeply.
  • Risk: weaker overview unless paired with a compact index.
