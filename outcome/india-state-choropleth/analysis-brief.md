# Data Analyst Brief: India Literacy Choropleth

## Analytical Direction

Use the map as a regional comparison of literacy-rate estimates across Indian states and union territories.

## Pattern Summary

- The values span roughly the mid-60s to just under 100 percent.
- Mizoram and Kerala sit near the top of the distribution.
- Andhra Pradesh and Arunachal Pradesh are among the lower values in this selected source.
- Several smaller union territories have high values, but their small populations and administrative structure make them different from larger states.

## Candidate Story Directions

### Selected: Regional Literacy Variation

- Claim or question: Where do literacy rates stand across India?
- Evidence: The state/UT estimates vary materially, making a choropleth appropriate.
- Why it matters: A rate-based metric allows fairer geographic comparison than raw literate-population counts.
- Caveat: The map compares levels, not causes.
- Suggested form: Single choropleth with tap/readout interaction.

### Alternative: High-Literacy Outliers

- Claim or question: Which regions sit far above the national pattern?
- Evidence: Kerala, Mizoram, and some compact territories are visibly high.
- Caveat: Requires additional context before claiming policy or social explanations.

### Alternative: Low-End Concentration

- Claim or question: Which regions lag in the selected estimate?
- Evidence: Several states fall in the lower part of the range.
- Caveat: Needs trend data or education-access variables to explain why.

## Handoff To Narrator

Use an explanatory frame. Keep the copy about the metric and comparison, not instructions for interaction or political-boundary implementation details.
