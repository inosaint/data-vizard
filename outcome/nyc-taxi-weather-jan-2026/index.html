January 25 Stopped NYC’s Yellow Taxis

One snow day, mapped from citywide demand to airport disruption

On one snowy Sunday, the taxi map thinned out.

January 25 had the month’s heaviest snowfall and its lowest yellow taxi count: 43,563 valid trips. The busiest January days topped 140,000.

69%lower than Jan 30, the month peak
24%below normal for snow hours by hour of day

The steepest drop was at the airports.

LaGuardia pickup activity fell from an average of about 2,697 trips to 100. JFK fell from about 4,645 to 781. That is the clearest spatial disruption in the taxi zones.

The drop was not uniform.

Brooklyn fell less boroughwide, and some neighborhood pickup zones were comparatively stable or even above baseline. A weather event changes the city unevenly.

How to read this

  • Taxi data is NYC TLC Yellow Taxi trip records for January 2026.
  • Weather is Open-Meteo modeled historical weather for NYC coordinates.
  • Zone drops compare January 25 against average January daily pickups excluding January 25.
  • This shows association and spatial contrast, not proof that snow alone caused every change.
