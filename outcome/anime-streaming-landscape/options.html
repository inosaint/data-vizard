Anime Shelf, Refined

These options all keep the local explorer feel, but each one emphasizes a different kind of browsing: immersive posters, a balanced reading desk, or a more structured availability view.

A. Shelf Wall

Poster-first browser with overview cues

A cinematic wall of posters leads the page, with a compact filter rail and small summary chips for decade, platform reach, and genre mix. Selecting a card opens a richer detail drawer with synopsis and platform links.

  • Best for: exploratory browsing and visual appeal.
  • Rendering: plain HTML, CSS, and lightweight JS.
  • Risk: still the least analytical of the three, even with the added overview cues.
B. Library Desk

Split explorer with big detail panel

A compact searchable list sits on the left while one selected title takes over the right side with poster, synopsis, platform badges, and context callouts. This balances browsing and reading without feeling like a spreadsheet.

  • Best for: a calm balance of browsing and reading.
  • Rendering: plain HTML, CSS, and lightweight JS.
  • Risk: shows fewer titles at once than the other two.
C. Availability Atlas

Platform-and-decade shelf bands

Titles are arranged in horizontal bands by platform reach or decade, so the page still feels like a shelf but makes the catalog structure more visible. Opening a title reveals the same rich detail view as the other options.

  • Best for: seeing catalog structure without losing the shelf feeling.
  • Rendering: plain HTML, CSS, and lightweight JS.
  • Risk: a little more abstract than the other two, so the grouping logic has to stay very clear.
