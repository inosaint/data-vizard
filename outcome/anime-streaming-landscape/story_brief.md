# Story Brief: A Browseable Anime Shelf

## Narrative Frame

Balanced exploratory piece with minimal copy. The page should help someone browse the catalog, notice platform concentration, and surface strong watch-now candidates without pretending the dataset is a full market census.

## Audience

Someone exploring anime locally who wants to filter by platform, era, genre, and rating without reading a long explainer first.

## Main Question

What does this catalog look like when you browse it as a shelf instead of a spreadsheet?

## Posture

- `Balanced`
- `minimal copy`

## Main Claim

This dataset is strongest as a local exploration tool: it combines title metadata, broad genre tags, approximate ratings, and platform links well enough to browse patterns and recommendations, even though some fields are too synthetic for hard market claims.

## Evidence Sequence

1. Establish the catalog shape: 206 titles, mostly from the 2010s and 2020s.
2. Show platform concentration: Crunchyroll dominates presence inside this file.
3. Let the user browse by decade, genre, and platform rather than forcing a single headline ranking.
4. Surface a few highly rated, multi-platform titles as easy starting points.
5. Keep caveats visible: this is a curated catalog snapshot, not the whole market.

## Text Budget

- One title
- One short subtitle
- One small methods or caveat block
- Brief labels for controls and detail panels

## Must-Keep Text

- A title that frames the artifact as a browseable shelf or local explorer
- A subtitle that explains the main controls
- A visible caveat that `popularity` and `rating` are approximate, heavily bucketed fields in this dataset
- A visible note that platform presence reflects this file, not full catalog share

## Optional Text

- Tiny callouts for top multi-platform picks
- A short “how to use this” line near the filters

## Annotation Priorities

- Make platform availability easy to scan
- Keep selected title details close to the poster and synopsis
- Use direct labels instead of extra narrative paragraphs
- Prefer short comparison text over editorial framing

## Designer Handoff

Build this more like a careful local media shelf than a dashboard. Filters and ranking matter, but the first impression should still feel visual and inviting. The interface should reward browsing one title at a time while still exposing bigger catalog patterns at a glance.
