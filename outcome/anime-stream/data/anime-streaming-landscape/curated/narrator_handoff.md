# Narrator Handoff

## Frame

Minimal, sharp, and unsentimental.

## Tone

- Keep visible text sparse.
- Let the image carry the first read.
- Put most metadata in hover tooltips rather than persistent copy.
- Prefer pressure, tension, and editorial precision over dreamy filler.
- If a line sounds like it is narrating the obvious, cut it.

## Story Spine

- Opening: one title only, with no subtitle unless it adds real information.
- Main observation: one-platform and two-platform titles form the dense body of the sky.
- Contrast: the rare three-platform and four-platform titles become standout stars.
- Caveat: this is a portrait of the provided dataset, not a full map of anime streaming rights.
- Takeaway: if a takeaway needs to be written out, keep it brutal and brief.

## Copy Guidance

- Prefer one strong title. Default to no subtitle.
- Avoid atmospheric filler, pseudo-poetic scene-setting, or "hover to explore" style instructions.
- Avoid generic data-art copy such as "suspended as stars," "read the sky," "revealed by the data," or similar self-describing lines.
- Avoid repeating what the viewer can already see.
- If text is needed, make it specific, cold, and earned.
- Avoid long annotations unless they clarify a highlighted title.
- Use tooltips for title, year, rating, platform count, genres, and platform names.

## Required Caveat Language

Platform availability reflects the titles in this dataset and should not be read as complete regional licensing data.
