# Analyst Handoff

## Selected Direction

Constellation of availability.

## Core Observation

Most anime titles in this dataset sit on 1 or 2 streaming platforms, while a small minority spread across 3 or 4 services. Those rare multi-platform titles make strong visual anchors.

## Evidence

- `streaming_platforms_count` distribution:
  - 1 platform: 53 titles
  - 2 platforms: 101 titles
  - 3 platforms: 45 titles
  - 4 platforms: 7 titles
- Average rating by platform count:
  - 1 platform: 8.15
  - 2 platforms: 8.25
  - 3 platforms: 8.20
  - 4 platforms: 8.69
- Dominant overlap pairs:
  - Crunchyroll + Funimation: 73
  - Crunchyroll + Hulu: 68
  - Crunchyroll + Netflix: 45
- High-rated multi-platform anchors include:
  - `Attack on Titan: The Final Season Part 2` (2022, rating 9.3, 3 platforms)
  - `Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood` (2009, rating 9.1, 4 platforms)
  - `Code Geass: Lelouch of the Rebellion R2` (2008, rating 9.0, 4 platforms)

## Why It Matters

This direction supports a data-art piece because the structure already suggests a spatial metaphor: a dense cloud of ordinary availability with a few bright, widely available standouts.

## Caveats

- Platform columns likely reflect title presence, not complete regional licensing.
- The relationship between rating and platform count is weak to modest, not causal.
- The dataset is curated and finite, so the piece should read as a portrait of this collection, not the whole anime market.

## Suggested Visual Forms

- Radial or orbital field
- Constellation map with cluster emphasis
- Dense point cloud with highlighted overlap hubs
