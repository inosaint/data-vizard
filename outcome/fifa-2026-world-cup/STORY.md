# Story brief

## Chosen direction

Show every 2026 World Cup group-stage match as a small football ground. A metric toggle changes the pitch layer so the reader can compare possession, shots, cards, fouls, corners, crosses, interceptions, and offsides without losing the match context.

## Frame

Comparative and investigative. The central question is: which match metrics move with the result, and which ones are just match texture?

## Main evidence

- Shots on target are the clearest result-aligned metric in this file: the team with more shots on target won 41 of 66 non-tied metric comparisons.
- Possession and total shots lean toward winners, but with more exceptions.
- Crosses, fouls, and cards often do not point toward the winner.

## Caveats

- The dataset covers the group stage only: 72 matches from June 11, 2026 through June 27, 2026.
- Groups are inferred from match pairings because the source file does not include an explicit group column.
- Possession values are rounded and sometimes sum to 101.
- Offsides are missing in 3 matches.

## Interface copy

Title: `Every Group Match, Read Through the Pitch`

Subtitle: `Toggle a metric to redraw all 72 group-stage matches as football grounds. Outcome markers stay fixed, so the same grid can show when possession, pressure, discipline, or chance quality moves with the score.`
