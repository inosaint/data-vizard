# Story Brief: Bangalore's Rain Has Two Peaks

## Selected Direction

- Analyst direction: `Bangalore's Rain Has Two Peaks, Not One`
- Posture: `Explanatory`
- Language load: `guided narrative`

## Narrative Frame

This should read as a climate explanation, not a forecast. The visualization needs to correct the oversimplified idea of one monsoon hump by showing a pre-monsoon build, a wet mid-year run, and an October peak that stands above the rest.

## Main Question

What does Bangalore's long-run rainfall cycle actually look like across the year?

## Main Observation

Bangalore does not move through a single smooth rainy season. Rain begins building before the core monsoon months, stays high through mid-year, and then reaches its strongest aggregate peak in October.

## Supporting Evidence

1. `October` has the highest long-run monthly rainfall total in the climatology: `6276.40`.
2. `July`, `August`, and `September` are all very wet, but each stays below October in total rainfall.
3. `May` is already materially rainy at `5631.40`, showing a strong pre-monsoon build.
4. `January` and `February` are comparatively dry, at `285.01` and `298.68`.

## Caveats

- This is a long-run historical climatology built from hourly records, not a forecast.
- The shape explains typical seasonal structure better than year-to-year variability.
- `2026` is partial coverage and should not drive the main seasonal view.
